# Chapter 2: Trigonometry

## Overview

In the first half of this chapter, the three trigonometric functions (sine, cosine and tangent) will be viewed as functions of angles. The second half of the chapter will approach trigonometry as functions of real numbers.

## Key Concepts

### The Unit Circle

The convention for measuring angles begins from the positive $x$-axis and rotates a point from $(1,0)$ counter-clockwise through $(x,y)$. This traces out an angle, $\theta$.

- The unit circle has a radius of 1
- When the arc length from $(1,0)$ to $(x,y)$ equals the radius, the angle $\theta = 1$ radian
- There are $2\pi$ radians in one complete rotation
- Positive direction: anticlockwise
- Negative direction: clockwise

### Degrees and Radians Conversion

$$1 \text{ revolution} = 2\pi \text{ radians} = 360°$$

Or equivalently:

$$\pi \text{ radians} = 180°$$

**Conversion formulas:**
- Degrees to radians: multiply by $\frac{\pi}{180}$
- Radians to degrees: multiply by $\frac{180}{\pi}$

### Arc Length

For a circle with radius $r$ and central angle $\theta$:

- If $\theta$ is in radians: $\text{Arc length} = r\theta$
- If $\theta$ is in degrees: $\text{Arc length} = \frac{\theta}{360°} \times 2\pi r$

### Trigonometric Ratios

For a right-angled triangle with angle $\theta$:

$$\sin(\theta) = \frac{\text{opposite}}{\text{hypotenuse}}$$

$$\cos(\theta) = \frac{\text{adjacent}}{\text{hypotenuse}}$$

$$\tan(\theta) = \frac{\text{opposite}}{\text{adjacent}} = \frac{\sin(\theta)}{\cos(\theta)}$$

### Special Angles

| Angle | $0$ | $\frac{\pi}{6}$ | $\frac{\pi}{4}$ | $\frac{\pi}{3}$ | $\frac{\pi}{2}$ |
|-------|-----|-----------------|-----------------|-----------------|-----------------|
| $\sin$ | $0$ | $\frac{1}{2}$ | $\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}$ | $\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}$ | $1$ |
| $\cos$ | $1$ | $\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}$ | $\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}$ | $\frac{1}{2}$ | $0$ |
| $\tan$ | $0$ | $\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}$ | $1$ | $\sqrt{3}$ | undefined |

## Formulas

### Pythagorean Identity
$$\sin^2(\theta) + \cos^2(\theta) = 1$$

### Double Angle Formulas
$$\sin(2\theta) = 2\sin(\theta)\cos(\theta)$$

$$\cos(2\theta) = \cos^2(\theta) - \sin^2(\theta) = 2\cos^2(\theta) - 1 = 1 - 2\sin^2(\theta)$$

### Sum and Difference Formulas
$$\sin(A \pm B) = \sin(A)\cos(B) \pm \cos(A)\sin(B)$$

$$\cos(A \pm B) = \cos(A)\cos(B) \mp \sin(A)\sin(B)$$

### Law of Sines
$$\frac{a}{\sin(A)} = \frac{b}{\sin(B)} = \frac{c}{\sin(C)}$$

### Law of Cosines
$$c^2 = a^2 + b^2 - 2ab\cos(C)$$

## Learning Objectives

1. Convert between degrees and radians
2. Calculate arc lengths and sector areas
3. Evaluate trigonometric functions for any angle using the unit circle
4. Apply trigonometric identities to simplify expressions
5. Solve trigonometric equations
6. Use the sine and cosine rules to solve triangles
